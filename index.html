About me

3rd year PhD student

I am in the 3rd and last year of a PhD program at the University of Montpellier in the south of France (a few kilometers away from the mediterranean sea). I work at the Laboratoire Univers et Particules de Montpellier (LUPM) under the supervision of Dr. Julien Lavalle on Dark Matter phenomenology.

When I am not working I enjoy skiing, running, cycling, swimming or watching sport on TV (judo, biathlon, rugby). In the past I practiced judo for 16 years, dance for 4 years and handball for 1 year. I love to cook (pastries only!) and I (try to) play guitar. Lately I have also tried my hand at photography.

Research

Dark Matter: Theoretical Cosmology, Particle physics, Astrophysics, Gravitational dynamics

My work is theoretical and focused on dark matter, one of the most puzzling mystery of the Universe, and its structuring at the (sub)galactic scale. My various projects are at the crossroad of different fields of high energy physics, from particle physics in the early Universe down to galactic dynamics through cosmological evolution of the matter density field and astrophysical observations.
